Classic rumor differs from gossip and urban legend, though these differences often blur. Gossip is evaluative idle talk about individuals – usually not present –often shared for amusement, to communicate social mores, and to exclude some-one from a social group (Gluckman, 1963; Rosnow & Fine, 1976; Smith, Lucas, &Latkin, 1999). An example of gossip: “Did you hear that Sally is having an affair?”When we object “That’s gossip!” we are saying that it slanders and it is idle talk;when we object “That’s rumor!” we mean that it is unverified (Sabini & Silver,1982). Rumor is a morsel of food in an information famine; gossip is a tasty juicytreat at a cocktail party.
